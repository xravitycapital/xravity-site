Minimalist design is not about stripping everything away — it is about leaving what matters.

## Core Principles

1. **Purpose over decoration** — every element must earn its place
2. **Whitespace is a feature** — it gives content room to breathe
3. **Typography leads** — great type does the heavy lifting
4. **Color with intention** — a restrained palette creates focus
5. **Remove friction** — eliminate unnecessary steps and choices

## Why Simplicity Wins

In a world of infinite distraction, the designs that succeed are the ones that help users focus. Minimalism isn't an aesthetic choice — it's a functional one.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

Start with less. Your users will thank you.
